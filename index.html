The Chaser App

Discover most reviewed cocktails near you and cocktail recipes in a way you’ve never experienced before. Our app provides you with an encyclopedia of the hottest cocktails of the season from bars and restaurants in your neighborhood. Don’t want to go out? No problem, Chaser app also offers a cocktail recipes with step by step instructions on how to make it at home!

Main roles: research, user empathy, ideation. Team of 5 people

Protopersona, User Interviews, affinity maps, feature prioritizatoin map, user flow map, storyboard, Wireframe Sketches, Low-fi and High-fi sketches, user tests of wireframes,

Phase 1
Research

We conducted user interviews using Zoom and a google survey to get qualitative and quantitative data to best understand who our ideal user was. The interview questions and objectives were based on an initial proto-persona as a way to gain insight into what types of questions and conversations we should be having in interviews. The google survey was a sure way to an overall understanding of what typical users look for when deciding where to go to get a cocktail. The survey results were then synthesized with the interviews to understand the opportunities present for problems that people have. Following the interviews, a number of interviews raised questions about the competitors in the market, which lead us to ask questions about potential holes that the Chaser App could fill.

Problem Statement

The Chaser App was designed to achieve an easier way for people to find new bars and cocktails to try. From our user research, current apps don't allow for people to easily find their coktail of choice and tailor their social life to it. How might we create an app that helps to curate an experience that users want to enjoy with others?.

Ideation

We thought of different features through the "I like, I wish, What if" method to come up with possible ideas for the app. Since we knew who the person using this app was, as well as the competitor opportunities, we created a feature list that then got ranked for the initial design of the app.
